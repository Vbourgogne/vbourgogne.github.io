Dump'n'Bump

Reflexes/Strength

 

A fun and physical bar game in which 4 players compete to get all the balls.

 

The project : Designed for an exercise in which we had to create a game playable without a keyboard, a mouse or a gamepad. The 4 persons with whom I worked with and myself went for a bar game, enjoyable to play with friends and unknown people alike, putting them in touch with one another.

The final result is a game for 2 to 4 players, with controllers that can move all around the circle-shaped board. At the beginning of a game, balls are dropped at the center of the board and players have to get more pellets than every other player to win. In order to get the balls,  the players can tilt the board by pushing it down, because it rests only on a small area at the center, but they can also push each other or use the flippers attached to their controllers to get the balls within reach.

 

What I learned : Designing this game really was a peculiar experience, due to its very physical and material nature, more than any other game I designed, which led me to learn how to make a prototype with limited resources. Furthermore, our production pipeline led us to really break down the different steps : we went from a simple sketch on a notebook to a 3D model made with Solidworks, before making a cardboard prototype and then, finally the final thing made in wood. Each and every one of these steps led us to modify the mechanics, adapting the game to the constraints in order to transliterate our intentions.
